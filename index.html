Week 8

This week we had to attend a talk at the Festival of Economics and create to graphs refutting or consolidating an argument the speakers talked about. i went to the talk about big data and I was interested in what Arthur Turrell from the ONS mentionned about inflation measures. He argued that the poorest people experience a much higher level of inflation as they buy a different basket of goods than the one looked at by the ONS when calculating the level of inflation. I wanted to look at a graph showing the evolution of the price of different baskets fo goods, indexed for April 2021, and then at another graph showing by how much different food and non-alcoholic beverages' prices grew since April 2021.

We can see that whilst the lowest cost grocery basket inflation is in line with the other two measures, it is usually above the simple 'Food and Non-alcoholic beverages' basket. This is exacerbated by the fact that the lowest-income households usually have to save money in order to afford essentials like food, heating, rent. Therefore, they will feel this inflation more than other households.

The second chart shows that most points are concentrated around the 0 to 0.15 rate of price change. The red dot shows the 12-month rate of inflation, which is also in the same area as most are concentrated in. We do not really observe a relation between the price of that item in April 2021 and the growth rate of its price, but we can see that quite a few of the items with high rates of change belong to the same food groups.
