Week 2

This week we looked at an FT chart showing total IMF lending in billions of USD from 1984 to today. In class, we discussed the fact that it might not show an accurate picture of the situaton because of inflation. Therefore, we wanted to divide IMF lending by World GDP to get a better sense of when countries should've gotten loans.

Whilst we may have thought that it would have been better to get a loan from the IMF in 1984, we can now clearly see in the second chart that although the IMF is lending more, comparatively the world is in a much better state than it was in 1984.
